Youth Engineers Initiative

We create hands on engineering experiences for students from kindergarten through grade twelve. Learners build real projects, solve real problems, and grow as thoughtful builders in their communities.

Join a program See how it works

Mission

Access, agency, and impact. We bring practical engineering to every learner through community partners and student mentors. The approach is simple and proven. Design, build, test, reflect, share.

Programs

Flexible formats for schools, libraries, and youth centers.

Grades 3 to 5

Discovery labs

Short sessions that build intuition through quick design challenges. Wind lift, bridges, and circuits with safe, low cost kits.

Grades 6 to 8

Build days

One day or weekend events where teams complete a working prototype by the end of the day. Tools and safety training included.

Grades 9 to 12

Studio projects

Multi week deep dives. Examples include wind tunnel design, radio links, and environmental sensors with real data collection.

All ages

Mentor network

High school and college mentors guide teams, model engineering habits, and build leadership through service.

Educators

Teacher support

Plug and play lesson kits, pacing guides, and rubrics that align to standards while keeping a focus on craft and curiosity.

Community

Service projects

Design for good with city and nonprofit partners. Examples include beach sensors, library maker events, and repair cafe sessions.

Impact

A growing community of learners and mentors.
